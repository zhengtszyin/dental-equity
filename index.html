The Problem

Basic oral hygiene supplies are easy to overlook, but not everyone has consistent access to toothbrushes, toothpaste, floss, and dental kits.

Limited access to oral hygiene supplies can affect comfort, confidence, school attendance, and overall well-being. Dental Equity focuses on one practical step: helping basic supplies reach organizations already serving NYC communities.

About Dental Equity

Dental Equity is a student-led community project started to improve access to basic oral hygiene supplies across New York City. The project focuses on collecting sealed supplies from local donors and distributing them through community organizations that serve students, families, and shelter residents. Started by Tsz Yin Zheng, a NYC student interested in healthcare access and community service.

FAQ

Common donation questions

What items do you accept?

We accept sealed, unused, and unexpired toothbrushes, toothpaste, floss, dental kits, and oral hygiene sample packs.

Do you accept opened items?

No. For safety, we only accept sealed and unused items.

Can dental offices donate sample packs?

Yes. We accept sealed and unexpired sample packs from dental offices.

Can you pick up donations?

Yes, we can coordinate local pickup when possible.

Where do donations go?

Supplies are donated through NYC schools, shelters, food pantries, youth centers, and community organizations.

Do you accept clinical tools or prescription products?

No. We do not accept prescription products, clinical tools, dental instruments, medications, or anything requiring professional supervision.
